To whom it may concern:

As you are aware, the Pledge Nothing campaign to save WRVU FM’s radio broadcasting license continues. We are working to keep WRVU on the air through the commitment of donors, students, faculty, staff, and parents to donate $0.00 until WRVU is guaranteed a future of radio broadcasting.

This week we have been in contact with Robert Ackley, who is the newly hired General Manager of WRVU. He approached us with a Pledge Nothing pledge, and we spoke with him directly on his reasons behind pledging. He wanted his 8-year $0.00 pledge to be public, and he had this to say:

“My name is Robert Ackley, and I am the newly hired General Manager of WRVU. Already, I feel that my time working for the station has provided me with the sort of real-world, practical experience that is unlike any I have yet acquired from my classes. As an Arts & Science student, WRVU provides the next best thing after Blair for me to get into the music business.

WRVU represents a valuable opportunity for learning which would hardly exist if it reverted solely to an online stream. I fear that if sold, many students would cease to be interested in participating in WRVU, just as many have quit already as a result of the VSC Board’s recent actions. As a non-profit that claims not to be facing any immediate financial danger, VSC ought not to sell off its largest asset at such a detriment to student learning when education should be its primary goal.

It saddens me to see from Vanderbilt’s administration such little apparent interest in its students, particularly when we have already gathered nearly 2000 undergraduate student signatures in opposition to the sale. I urge the administration to take a stand on this issue, for otherwise, I pledge with these other supporters of WRVU not to donate to Vanderbilt for the next 8 years if WRVU’s broadcast license is sold.”

People who know the value of WRVU radio continue to come forth to stand behind the station.
